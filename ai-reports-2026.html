Finding 1

Coding dominates

Computer and math tasks — 36% of all conversations. Top 10 tasks out of 3,000 cover 24% of usage. Most common: "fixing software errors" — 6% of all usage.

Finding 2

Augmentation overtook automation

52% of conversations are augmentation (iterating with AI), 45% automation (delegating). People use Claude as a thinking partner, not just an executor. In the API, automation still dominates.

Finding 3

Prompting is a skill with real ROI

0.92 correlation between prompt complexity and output quality. The 12x speed gain is not universal — it belongs to those who can write sophisticated prompts. This creates a two-tier AI economy within organizations.

Finding 4

Geographic inequality

US, India, Japan, UK, South Korea lead. Usage strongly correlates with GDP per capita. Wealthier nations capture disproportionately more value.

Finding 5 — India

15x speed gain

India = 5.8% of total Claude usage. Speed gain: 15x (vs 12x globally). 45.2% of usage — software development tasks. #1 in the world by this metric.

Theory vs reality

Enormous untapped potential

AI is theoretically capable across Management, Legal, Education — but real usage is far more modest. Office & admin is the surprise leader. The gap = untapped potential.
